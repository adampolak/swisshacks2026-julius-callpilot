## Application rules (for the model)

1. **Explicit listing wins.** A country named in a specific tier takes precedence over any blanket rule. This resolves the EU overlap: although the EU blanket sits in Tier 1, **Hungary, Greece, Bulgaria, and Romania are explicitly Tier 2**, so they are Tier 2.
2. **Unlisted ⇒ Tier 3.** If a country appears in neither Tier 1 nor Tier 2, classify it Tier 3. By this rule, notable Tier 3 examples include **mainland China, Russia, and the United Kingdom** (none are listed). Do not "correct" the framework from outside knowledge — apply it as written.
3. **Hong Kong and Taiwan are Tier 1; mainland China is Tier 2.** Treat them separately.
4. **Never guess.** If a tier is not derivable from this reference, do not assign one.
